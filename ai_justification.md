# 2. Why AI

## Why Traditional Filters and UX Cannot Solve This
Traditional UX relies on deterministic facets (Price, Brand, Age). However, a mother's constraints are highly contextual, multi-dimensional, and often expressed implicitly. A filter cannot understand: *"I live in a 5th-floor walkup and need something for my 3-month-old that my husband can also jog with."* Building a GUI with dropdowns for every possible life scenario (elevator size, jogging frequency, boot space) results in an overwhelmingly complex interface that users abandon.

## What AI Capability is Essential
The essential capability is **Semantic Synthesis and Multi-Constraint Optimization**. The AI can interpret messy, unstructured natural language, cross-reference it against structured product data, and synthesize a personalized justification. It translates a product spec ("Weight: 12kg, Fold: 63cm") into an empathetic reality check ("This stroller is too heavy to carry up to your 5th-floor walkup alone").

## What Breaks Without AI
Without AI, the burden of translation remains entirely on the user. The platform provides raw data (specs, prices), and the mother must mentally map that data to her life. When cognitive overload hits, the decision process breaks down. She either buys the cheapest option, buys nothing, or switches to a competitor who happens to have a blog post that exactly describes her situation. Without AI, the "expert consultation" experience cannot scale.
