# 1. Problem Definition

## Persona
**Aisha (28)**, a first-time expectant mother living in a 2-bedroom apartment in Dubai. She works full-time, has high purchasing power but extremely low time and domain expertise. She is highly anxious about safety, child development, and making a "wrong" expensive purchase (like a car seat or stroller). 

## 3 Concrete Pain Points
1. **Decision Paralysis from Homogeneous Options:** When she searches for a "stroller", she sees 200+ results with identical marketing jargon ("lightweight", "premium", "safe") but cannot decode which one actually fits her specific lifestyle (e.g., small elevator, no car).
2. **Low Trust in Reviews and Filters:** She suspects 5-star reviews are fake or irrelevant ("Great stroller!" doesn't help if the reviewer lives in a villa and she lives in a flat). Standard e-commerce filters (Brand, Price, Color) do not map to her real-world constraints (e.g., "fits in overhead bin" or "safe for preemies").
3. **High Cost of Mistakes:** Buying the wrong feeding bottle is an AED 80 mistake; buying the wrong travel system is an AED 3,000 mistake that is a nightmare to return once unboxed.

## Why This is High-Leverage
Solving this problem specifically targets **cart abandonment at the consideration phase**. For high-ticket items (gear, furniture), mothers spend days researching across multiple browser tabs and often abandon the funnel because they cannot confidently decide. A tool that acts as an expert consultant and cuts through the noise will directly increase high-ticket conversion rates, build long-term brand loyalty (trust), and dramatically reduce the cost of returns caused by mismatched product expectations.
